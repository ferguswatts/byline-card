Background

Andrea Vance is a Northern Irish political journalist currently serving as a senior journalist at Stuff in Wellington. She grew up near Belfast, where her father edited three local newspapers. She worked for seven years as an investigative reporter at the British tabloid News of the World. When the phone hacking scandal broke, Vance was in the Glasgow newsroom and has denied any personal involvement, stating "no one had any idea it was going on." She subsequently worked as night news editor at The Scotsman before emigrating to New Zealand with her Kiwi husband. She became a prominent press gallery journalist, working for Fairfax/Stuff and later 1News. In 2013, she was at the centre of the Peter Dunne email scandal, in which minister Dunne resigned after refusing to release 86 email exchanges with Vance regarding a leaked GCSB report. Her phone records and parliamentary swipe-card data were improperly provided to the inquiry without her knowledge, prompting a major privacy breach complaint. She was named Political Journalist of the Year in 2025.

Background

Barry Soper (born c. 1951, Gore) is the longest-serving member of New Zealand's Parliamentary Press Gallery, having joined in 1980. After briefly attending the Royal New Zealand Police College in 1969, he entered journalism at The Southland Times, progressing through the Mataura Ensign, Otago Daily Times, New Zealand Truth, and Waikato Times. He joined the press gallery working for APN before moving to Newstalk ZB, where he has served as political editor and senior political correspondent. He has covered eleven prime ministers, both Fijian coups (1987 and 2000), and Nelson Mandela's inauguration. He was named Individual Radio Journalist of the Year in 2001. In 1992, he was approached to stand as a Labour Party candidate in the Wellington Central by-election but declined. He married journalist and broadcaster Heather du Plessis-Allan in 2009. He was named unfavourably in Jacinda Ardern's 2025 memoir.
